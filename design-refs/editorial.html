The Lede

A developer building in public, between roles and shipping proof.

Hello - I'm Victor. I build software for the web: clean APIs, interfaces that feel fast, and small tools I would actually want to use myself. This page is less a brochure than a working record - a dispatch from the desk.

The headline below is the part most résumés hide: an honest, versioned log of how I've spent the last year. I treated time between roles as a build season, not a holding pattern, and the changelog is the receipt.

If you're hiring for a backend or full-stack role on a team that values shipping and craft in equal measure, read on - and then say hello. I read everything that isn't a mass-blast.

Field Notes

No. 01

Debugging by bisection

Don't read every line. Cut the problem in half, test, repeat. A bug in 1,000 lines is ten halvings from caught - the same trick git bisect runs on your history.

No. 02

The five-minute rule

Before asking anyone, spend five minutes writing what you expected, what happened, and what you tried. Half the time, that is the fix.
